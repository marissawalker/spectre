Detailed Description

Circularly polarized Alfvén wave solution in Minkowski spacetime travelling in the z-direction.

An analytic solution to the 3-D GrMhd system. The user specifies the wavenumber \(k_z\) of the Alfvén wave, the constant pressure throughout the fluid \(P\), the constant rest mass density throughout the fluid \(\rho_0\), the adiabatic index for the ideal fluid equation of state \(\gamma\), the background magnetic field strength \(B_0\), and the strength of the perturbation of the magnetic field \(B_1\). The Alfvén wave phase speed is then given by:

\[v_A = \frac{B_0}{\sqrt{\rho_0 h + B_0^2}}\]

The amplitude of the fluid velocity is given by:

\[v_f = \frac{-B_1}{\sqrt{\rho_0 h + B_0^2}}\]

In Cartesian coordinates \((x, y, z)\), and using dimensionless units, the primitive quantities at a given time \(t\) are then

\begin{align*} \rho(\vec{x},t) &= \rho_0 \\ v_x(\vec{x},t) &= v_f \cos(k_z(z - v_A t))\\ v_y(\vec{x},t) &= v_f \sin(k_z(z - v_A t))\\ v_z(\vec{x},t) &= 0\\ P(\vec{x},t) &= P, \\ \epsilon(\vec{x}, t) &= \frac{P}{(\gamma - 1)\rho_0}\\ B_x(\vec{x},t) &= B_1 \cos(k_z(z - v_A t))\\ B_y(\vec{x},t) &= B_1 \sin(k_z(z - v_A t))\\ B_z(\vec{x},t) &= B_0 \end{align*}
