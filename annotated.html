 CSettleToConstantGiven an initial function \(f(t)\) and its first two derivatives at the matching time \(t_0\), the constant coefficients \(A,B,C\) are computed such that the resulting function of time \(g(t)\) satisfies \(g(t=t_0)=f(t=t_0)\) and approaches a constant value for \(t > t_0\) on a timescale of \(\tau\). The resultant function is

\[ g(t) = A + (B+C(t-t_0)) e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau} \]

where \(\tau\)=decay_time and \(t_0\)=match_time
